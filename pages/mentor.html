Mentorship

In college and looking to get a job as a developer? Are you underrepresented in the tech industry? I'd like to help.

The college placement system today is broken

Universities lean more and more on GPA and aptitude tests to filter out candidates for companies instead of helping students learn what actual software development is like. The few students who do stand out during placements are usually those who worked on projects outside of their academics, usually with very little encouragement or support from universities. Like with everything else in the world, this too disproportionately impacts women and other minorities.

I was fortunate to have had professors and friends who encouraged me to keep doing things outside of just exam preparations, and that was the single biggest factor in helping me get a head start when I graduated. Without getting too philosophical, I'm hoping this one small way of trying to pay that forward.
